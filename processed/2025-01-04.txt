1. **Translation**:
食べる量は十分だったんですか？
大丈夫ですか？
手伝えることは何ですか？

2. **Romaji**:
Taberu ryou wa juubun dattan desu ka?
Daijoubu desu ka?
Tasukaeru koto wa nan desu ka?

3. **Sentence Breakdown**:
- 食べる量は十分だったんですか？(Taberu ryou wa juubun dattan desu ka?)
  - 食べる (taberu) - verb, plain positive form
  - 量 (ryou) - noun meaning "amount"
  - は (wa) - topic marking particle
  - 十分だった (juubun datta) - i-adjective "enough" in past tense
  - んですか (n desu ka) - casual form of explanatory sentence ending particle か
  - This sentence is asking if the amount eaten was enough.

- 大丈夫ですか？(Daijoubu desu ka?)
  - 大丈夫 (daijoubu) - noun/adjective meaning "alright, okay"
  - です (desu) - copula
  - か (ka) - question marking particle
  - This sentence is a simple question asking if the person is alright.

- 手伝えることは何ですか？(Tasukaeru koto wa nan desu ka?)
  - 手伝える (tasukaeru) - potential form of verb "to help"
  - こと (koto) - nominalizer making the verb "help" into a noun
  - は (wa) - topic marking particle
  - 何 (nan) - interrogative pronoun "what"
  - です (desu) - copula
  - か (ka) - question marking particle
  - This sentence inquires about what can be done to help.

4. **Vocabulary List**:
- 食べる (taberu) - verb, to eat
- 量 (ryou) - noun, amount
- 十分 (juubun) - i-adjective, enough
- 大丈夫 (daijoubu) - noun/adjective, alright, okay
- 手伝える (tasukaeru) - verb, to help
- こと (koto) - noun, thing
- 何 (nan) - pronoun, what

5. **Original English Text**:
Did you eat enough?
Are you feeling alright?
What can I do to help?
