1. **Translation**:
料理してくれてありがとう。
彼は元気ですか？
十分な水を飲みましたか？

2. **Romaji**:
Ryouri shite kurete arigatou.
Kare wa genki desu ka?
Jyubun na mizu o nomimashita ka?

3. **Sentence Breakdown**:
   - 料理してくれてありがとう。 (Ryouri shite kurete arigatou.)
     - 料理 (ryouri): noun meaning "cooking"
     - して (shite): te-form of the verb "suru" meaning "to do"
     - くれて (kurete): te-form of the verb "kureru" meaning "to do for (me)"
     - ありがとう (arigatou): interjection meaning "thank you"

   - 彼は元気ですか？ (Kare wa genki desu ka?)
     - 彼 (kare): pronoun meaning "he"
     - は (wa): topic particle
     - 元気 (genki): noun meaning "healthy; energetic"
     - です (desu): copula, polite form of "to be"
     - か (ka): question marker

   - 十分な水を飲みましたか？ (Jyubun na mizu o nomimashita ka?)
     - 十分な (jyubun na): na-adjective meaning "enough"
     - 水 (mizu): noun meaning "water"
     - を (o): object particle
     - 飲みました (nomimashita): past tense of the verb "nomu" meaning "to drink"
     - か (ka): question marker

4. **Vocabulary List**:
   - 料理 (ryouri): cooking (noun)
   - して (shite): te-form of "suru" - to do (verb)
   - ありがとう (arigatou): thank you (interjection)
   - 彼 (kare): he (pronoun)
   - 元気 (genki): healthy; energetic (noun)
   - メール (meiru): email (noun)
   - 十分な (jyubun na): enough (na-adjective)
   - 水 (mizu): water (noun)
   - 飲む (nomu): to drink (verb)

5. **Original English Text**:
Thank you for cooking.
How is he doing?
Did you drink enough water?
